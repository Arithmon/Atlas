**Claim.** Large families of compact G2 manifolds can be built by gluing two
asymptotically cylindrical Calabi-Yau halves along a common K3 surface (the
twisted connected sum construction).

**Relation.** Divergent. The TCS program supplies most known compact G2
examples, and the founding framework's geometry provably lives outside it.

**Δ.** The Betti pair (21, 77) is excluded from all classical and
extra-twisted TCS constructions (an exhaustive no-go over 3852 building-block
configurations); GIFT's candidate geometry is a Donaldson-type coassociative
K3 fibration instead. The divergence here is a theorem, not a preference, and
the TCS toolbox (matching problems, K3 lattices) remains the shared language.
